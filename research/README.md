# AODS Research Archive

Non-normative research notes used to position AODS, select benchmark opponents, and track adjacent standards, tools, and research areas.

## Purpose

This folder exists to answer three questions:

1. **What problem space is AODS actually in?**
2. **Which external systems are meaningful comparison targets?**
3. **What should AODS prove against the outside world, not just against itself?**

## Archive contents

| File | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `2026-04-14-landscape-and-comparator-scan.md` | First multi-round scan of related research themes, influential open-source projects, and recommended benchmark opponents |

## Working rules

- Treat everything in `research/` as **non-normative**.
- Preserve dated scans instead of overwriting history.
- Record **why** a comparator matters, not just that it exists.
- When useful, include GitHub stars as a rough influence signal, but do not treat stars as the only measure of importance.
- Standards and older documentation ecosystems can be strategically important even when their GitHub metrics look small.
